Overview

RippleVMS coordinates community volunteers who monitor, document, and respond to activities in their communities. The system helps organizations manage volunteers across multiple zones.

Shift Scheduling

Manage shifts across 13 zones in Durham, Orange, and Wake counties

Training Coordination

Ensure volunteer preparedness through organized training sessions

Volunteer Management

Role-based access control for coordinators and volunteers

Schedule Coordination

Weekly views for dispatchers and zone leads

User Roles

The VMS has four user roles, each with different capabilities:

Volunteer

The base role for all community members participating in monitoring activities.

  • ✓ View and RSVP to available shifts
  • ✓ View and sign up for training sessions
  • ✓ Update personal profile and availability
  • ✓ View their own schedule and upcoming shifts
Coordinator

Zone and shift coordinators who manage volunteer activities.

  • ✓ Everything Volunteers can do, plus:
  • ✓ Create and manage shifts
  • ✓ View and manage shift rosters
  • ✓ Confirm or reject volunteer RSVPs
  • ✓ Access volunteer directory
  • ✓ Assign zone leads to shifts
  • ✓ Send email blasts to volunteers
Dispatcher

Has full coordinator access plus handles real-time coordination and ICE sighting management.

  • ✓ Everything Coordinators can do, plus:
  • ✓ Create and manage shifts and trainings
  • ✓ Access Coordinator Console
  • ✓ View schedule dashboard with dispatcher assignments
  • ✓ Coordinate field responses in real-time
  • ✓ Receive and manage ICE sighting reports
  • ✓ Update sighting status (verify, respond, close)
  • ✓ View sightings on map
Administrator

Full system access for organizational leadership.

  • ✓ Everything all other roles can do, plus:
  • ✓ Manage all users and their roles
  • ✓ Configure zones, shift types, training types, and qualified roles
  • ✓ Draw zone boundaries on coverage map
  • ✓ Bulk import volunteers
  • ✓ Access system settings

ICE Sightings

The ICE sighting system allows community members to report immigration enforcement activity and enables dispatchers to coordinate responses.

Reporting a Sighting (Public)

Anyone can report an ICE sighting using the public report form at /report. Reports use the SALUTE model:

Field Description Example
Size/Strength Number of officers and vehicles "5-6 officers, 2 white SUVs"
Activity What is happening "Officers approaching homes"
Location Where the activity is occurring "Parked in front of Wendy's on Main St"
Uniform What they're wearing "Blue vests with ICE on the back"
Time When it was observed Date and time picker
Equipment Vehicles and gear "Helmets, vests, and unmarked vehicles"

Reporters can optionally include their contact information for follow-up, photos/videos, and use their location or pin a spot on the map.

24/7 Hotline

For immediate reports, community members can also call the hotline: 336-543-0353

Viewing Sightings (Dispatchers/Coordinators)

Dispatchers and Coordinators can view and manage all sighting reports from the Sightings page:

  • List View - Browse all sightings with filters
  • Map View - See sightings plotted on a map with color-coded status markers
  • Filter by status - New, Reviewing, Verified, Responded, Closed

Sighting Statuses

Status Meaning
New Just submitted, awaiting review
Reviewing Dispatcher is evaluating the report
Verified Report has been confirmed as accurate
Responded Field team has been dispatched/responded
Closed Incident resolved or no longer active

Managing Sightings (Dispatchers/Coordinators)

  1. Click on a sighting to view full SALUTE details and any photos
  2. View the location on the embedded map
  3. Update the status as the response progresses
  4. Add internal notes for coordination
  5. Contact reporter if follow-up info was provided

Shifts

Viewing Shifts

The Shifts page shows all available and upcoming shifts. You can:

  • Filter by zone - Focus on specific geographic areas
  • Filter by shift type - Patrol, Collection, On-Call Field Support
  • Filter by date range - Find shifts for specific days
  • Search - Find shifts by title or description

Shift Types

Shift types are configured by administrators in Admin â†’ Shift Types. Common shift types include:

Type Description
Watch Brigade Community patrol shifts for area monitoring and presence
Collection Intelligence collection and monitoring shifts
On-Call Field Support On-call shifts for rapid response and field support

Note: Your organization may have different shift types configured. Each type has its own color for easy identification.

RSVPing to a Shift

  1. Browse available shifts on the Shifts page
  2. Click on a shift to view details
  3. Click "RSVP to this Shift"
  4. Your RSVP status will show as "Pending" until a coordinator confirms

RSVP Statuses

Status Meaning
Pending Your RSVP is awaiting coordinator approval
Confirmed You're confirmed for this shift
Declined Your RSVP was not accepted (capacity/requirements)
Cancelled You or a coordinator cancelled your participation

Creating Shifts (Dispatchers/Coordinators/Admins)

  1. Navigate to Shifts
  2. Click "Create Shift"
  3. Fill in: Title, Shift Type, Zone, Date & Time, Capacity, Description
  4. Click "Create Shift"

Managing Shift Rosters (Dispatchers/Coordinators/Admins)

  1. Click on any shift to view details
  2. Click "View Roster" or the roster icon
  3. From the roster page you can:
    • Confirm RSVPs - Approve pending volunteers
    • Decline RSVPs - Reject volunteers (with reason)
    • Assign Zone Lead - Designate shift leadership
    • Remove volunteers - Cancel confirmed participants
Bulk RSVP Confirmation:

Check boxes next to pending volunteers, then click "Confirm Selected" to approve multiple at once.

Bulk Shift Operations (Dispatchers/Coordinators/Admins)

Manage multiple shifts at once using the multi-select feature:

Selecting Shifts:
  • • Check the box next to individual shifts
  • • Use "Select All" checkbox in the table header to select all visible shifts
  • • A sticky toolbar appears at the bottom showing the count of selected shifts
Bulk Edit:
  1. Select shifts you want to modify
  2. Click "Edit Selected" in the toolbar
  3. Choose what to change:
    • Shift Type - Change to a different type (e.g., Watch Brigade, Collection)
    • Time Slot - Update start and end times
    • Volunteer Limits - Adjust min/max volunteer counts
  4. Click "Apply Changes"
Bulk Cancel:
  1. Select shifts you want to cancel
  2. Click "Cancel Selected" in the toolbar
  3. Confirm the cancellation
  4. All signed-up volunteers will be notified automatically via email

Trainings

Viewing Training Sessions

The Trainings page lists all available training sessions. Filter by:

  • Training type - Verifier, Zone Lead, Dispatcher
  • Date range - Upcoming sessions
  • Status - Sessions you've completed vs. available

Training Types & Qualified Roles

Training types are configured by administrators and can grant Qualified Roles upon completion. Default training types include:

Type Description Grants Qualified Role
Verifier Learn to verify and document incidents accurately Verifier
Zone Lead Leadership training for coordinating zone activities Zone Lead
Dispatcher Training for dispatch operations and communication Dispatcher

Note: Administrators can create additional training types and qualified roles in the Admin panel.

Signing Up for Training

  1. Browse available sessions on the Trainings page
  2. Click on a session to view details
  3. Click "RSVP" to register
  4. Attend the session at the scheduled time

Training Completion

  1. A coordinator or dispatcher marks your attendance as confirmed
  2. The training appears in your profile under "Completed Trainings"
  3. If the training grants a Qualified Role (e.g., Verifier, Zone Lead), you automatically receive that qualification
  4. You may now be eligible for shifts requiring that training or qualified role

Schedule

The Schedule page provides a weekly calendar view showing:

For All Users:

  • Your confirmed shifts for the week
  • Training sessions you're registered for

For Coordinators/Dispatchers/Admins:

  • Dispatch Coordinator Assignments - Who's the dispatch coordinator each day
  • Backup Dispatchers - Region-wide backup dispatchers available to support any county
  • Dispatcher Assignments - Who's on dispatch duty (display varies by mode)
  • Zone Lead Assignments - Zone leads assigned to each shift
  • Filter by zone to see specific area coverage

Backup Dispatchers

Backup dispatchers are region-wide and available to support any county during their assigned time blocks. They appear in an orange "Backup Disp." row on the schedule when assigned:

  • Shows backup dispatcher names organized by time block
  • Only visible when backup dispatchers are assigned for the week
  • Backup dispatchers can step in for any county as needed

Dispatcher Display by Mode

The Schedule page displays dispatchers differently based on the admin-configured Dispatcher Scheduling Mode:

Mode Activity Level Display
Regional Low A dedicated "Dispatcher (Regional)" section between Dispatch Coordinator and counties, one dispatcher per time block for all counties
County Medium A "Dispatcher" row under each county header, showing that county's dispatcher for each day
Zone High (default) Dispatchers appear inside each shift cell alongside zone assignments

Navigating the Schedule

  • Use ← Previous and Next → to change weeks
  • Click Today to return to the current week
  • Click any shift to view details
  • Click dispatcher cells/rows to assign or change dispatchers (Coordinators/Admins only)

The Admin section provides system configuration options.

Zones

Manage geographic zones: add, edit, or deactivate monitoring areas. Set zone colors and draw boundaries on the coverage map.

Shift Types

Configure available shift categories, names, descriptions, and colors.

Training Types

Manage training categories, set prerequisites, and configure which trainings grant qualified roles.

Qualified Roles

Manage customizable positions volunteers can fill during shifts (e.g., Verifier, Zone Lead, Dispatcher).

System Settings

Configure organization name, email notifications, defaults, and dispatcher scheduling mode.

Dispatcher Scheduling Mode

Control how dispatchers are assigned and displayed on the schedule grid based on your current activity level.

Configuring Dispatcher Mode

  1. Navigate to Admin → Settings
  2. Find the "Dispatcher Scheduling Mode" section
  3. Select the mode that matches your current activity level:
    • Regional - One dispatcher for all counties per time block (low activity weeks)
    • County - One dispatcher per county (medium activity weeks)
    • Zone - Dispatcher per county + time block cell (high activity, default)
  4. Changes take effect immediately on the Schedule page

Mode Switching Behavior

When switching between modes, existing dispatcher assignments are hidden but preserved. Switching back to a previous mode will restore those assignments. No data is lost when changing modes.

Managing Qualified Roles

Qualified Roles are customizable positions that volunteers can hold. They can be granted automatically through training completion or manually assigned by administrators.

Creating Qualified Roles

  1. Navigate to Admin → Qualified Roles
  2. Click "Add Qualified Role"
  3. Enter a name (e.g., "Field Medic")
  4. Add an optional description
  5. Choose a display color
  6. Click "Create"

Qualified Role Features

  • Auto-grant via Training - Link a training type to automatically grant a qualified role upon completion
  • Manual Assignment - Assign qualified roles directly to volunteers from the Volunteers page
  • Shift Requirements - Configure shift types to require specific qualified roles
  • Color Coding - Each role has a color for easy identification in the UI

Managing Zone Boundaries

Administrators can draw zone boundaries on a map to visualize coverage areas on the landing page.

Drawing Zone Boundaries

  1. Navigate to Admin → Zones
  2. Click the zone you want to map
  3. Click "Draw Map" to open the boundary editor
  4. Select the polygon tool from the top-right controls
  5. Click on the map to place vertices of your zone boundary
  6. Close the polygon by clicking on the first point
  7. Drag vertices to refine the shape
  8. Click "Save Boundaries" when done

Zone Display Settings

  • Color - Each zone can have a unique color for easy identification
  • Map Status - Shows "Drawn" or "Not set" indicating if boundaries exist
  • Coverage Map - Zone boundaries appear on the public landing page map

Common Workflows

Volunteer Signs Up for a Shift

  1. 1. Volunteer browses Shifts page
  2. 2. Volunteer clicks shift and RSVPs
  3. 3. If auto-confirm is enabled: Volunteer is automatically confirmed
  4. If auto-confirm is disabled:
  5. a. Coordinator sees pending RSVP on dashboard
  6. b. Coordinator opens shift roster and confirms the RSVP
  7. 4. Volunteer sees shift on their dashboard

Note: Auto-confirm can be enabled in Admin â†’ Settings

Coordinator Creates a Patrol Shift

  1. 1. Coordinator clicks "Create Shift"
  2. 2. Coordinator selects "Patrol" type
  3. 3. Coordinator chooses zone, date, time, capacity
  4. 4. Coordinator adds description with special instructions
  5. 5. Coordinator saves the shift
  6. 6. Shift appears for all volunteers to RSVP

Assigning a Zone Lead

  1. 1. Coordinator opens shift roster
  2. 2. Coordinator finds confirmed volunteer
  3. 3. Coordinator clicks "Assign as Zone Lead"
  4. 4. Zone Lead designation appears on schedule view
  5. 5. Zone Lead can see their assignment on dashboard

New Volunteer Onboarding

  1. 1. Admin creates volunteer account (or imports via CSV)
  2. 2. Volunteer logs in for first time
  3. 3. Volunteer sees welcome screen with overview
  4. 4. Volunteer completes required training (e.g., Verifier)
  5. 5. Volunteer can now RSVP to shifts requiring that training

Confirming Multiple RSVPs

  1. 1. Coordinator opens shift roster
  2. 2. Coordinator reviews pending RSVPs
  3. 3. Coordinator checks boxes for volunteers to confirm
  4. 4. Coordinator clicks "Confirm Selected"
  5. 5. All selected volunteers are confirmed at once

Reporting an ICE Sighting

  1. 1. Community member visits /report
  2. 2. Community member fills in SALUTE fields (Size, Activity, Location, Uniform, Time, Equipment)
  3. 3. Community member optionally adds photos and contact info
  4. 4. Community member submits the report
  5. 5. Dispatchers receive notification of new sighting
  6. 6. Sighting appears on dispatcher dashboard

Dispatcher Responds to ICE Sighting

  1. 1. Dispatcher sees new sighting on dashboard
  2. 2. Dispatcher clicks to view full details and location map
  3. 3. Dispatcher sets status to "Reviewing"
  4. 4. Dispatcher verifies report validity (calls reporter if needed)
  5. 5. Dispatcher sets status to "Verified"
  6. 6. Dispatcher coordinates field response if needed
  7. 7. Dispatcher sets status to "Responded" when team dispatched
  8. 8. Dispatcher sets status to "Closed" when incident resolved

Creating and Assigning Qualified Roles

  1. 1. Admin navigates to Admin → Qualified Roles
  2. 2. Admin clicks "Add Qualified Role"
  3. 3. Admin enters name (e.g., "Field Medic"), description, and color
  4. 4. Admin saves the new role
  5. 5. Admin optionally links it to a Training Type (in Training Types admin)
  6. 6. Admin can manually assign to volunteers via Volunteers page
  7. 7. Volunteers with the qualified role can now sign up for shifts requiring it

Setting Up Zone Map Coverage

  1. 1. Admin navigates to Admin → Zones
  2. 2. Admin clicks on a zone to edit
  3. 3. Admin sets the zone color
  4. 4. Admin clicks "Draw Map"
  5. 5. Admin uses polygon tool to trace zone boundaries
  6. 6. Admin saves boundaries
  7. 7. Zone now appears on landing page coverage map

Sending a Schedule Announcement

  1. 1. Coordinator clicks "Send Email Blast" on dashboard
  2. 2. Coordinator selects "Schedule Announcement" template
  3. 3. Coordinator reviews/edits the default message
  4. 4. Coordinator optionally filters by zone or role
  5. 5. Coordinator sets date range for shifts to include
  6. 6. Coordinator previews recipient count
  7. 7. Coordinator clicks "Send"
  8. 8. Each volunteer receives personalized email with their zone's shifts

Announcing New Training Sessions

  1. 1. Coordinator clicks "Send Email Blast" on dashboard
  2. 2. Coordinator selects "Training Announcement" template
  3. 3. Coordinator customizes the message about the trainings
  4. 4. Coordinator filters to volunteers who need the training (optional)
  5. 5. Coordinator sets date range for sessions to include
  6. 6. Coordinator sends the blast
  7. 7. Volunteers receive email with upcoming training table and RSVP link

Tips & Best Practices

For Volunteers

  • • Keep your profile updated with current contact info
  • • RSVP early - popular shifts fill up quickly
  • • Complete all required trainings to unlock more shift types
  • • Check your dashboard regularly for schedule changes

For Coordinators

  • • Review pending RSVPs daily to keep volunteers informed
  • • Use bulk confirmation to save time with multiple RSVPs
  • • Assign zone leads early so they can prepare
  • • Add clear descriptions to shifts with special requirements
  • • Use Schedule Announcement emails to notify volunteers of new shifts
  • • Training Announcement automatically includes upcoming sessions

For Dispatchers

  • • Check the schedule view at the start of each day
  • • Know which zone leads are on duty
  • • Keep contact info handy for active volunteers
  • • Monitor incoming sightings and update status promptly
  • • Use the map view to see sighting locations at a glance
  • • Add notes to sightings to document coordination efforts

For Administrators

  • • Regularly review volunteer status and engagement
  • • Keep zone and training configurations up to date
  • • Use bulk import for onboarding multiple volunteers
  • • Monitor system usage to identify needs
